Section 1

The first month is all about settling in. We’ll show you around our three main offices, in Westminster, Croydon and Sheffield. We’ll show you how designers work in government, how government works and talk a lot about user needs.

You’ll spend time with teams that are working on public facing services, like renew your passport and apply for a visa, and internal teams designing applications for staff. You’ll observe user research, as it the best way to understand the work we do.

You’ll read a lot.

Training at this stage

  • Home Office digital, data and technology induction
  • shadow user researchers
  • spend a day with a person from each profession: developers, delivery and product managers, business analysts
  • attend the User Research & Design team meetups
  • attend Interaction Design team meetings

You’ll have learnt

  • about the Home Office and what it does
  • about government as a whole
  • the digital service standards
  • how we do user-centred design in government
  • how agile projects are run
  • the different design specialisms and how they cross-over
